Rava Idli

Rava Idli is a popular South Indian dish made from semolina (rava) and is typically served as a breakfast or snack item. Here's a simple recipe along with the instruments you'll need:

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dietfood

instructions

Prepare the Batter:

Heat oil in a small frying pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter. In a mixing bowl, add semolina (rava), yogurt, water, salt, baking soda, and the tempering from the frying pan. Mix well to form a smooth batter. Ensure there are no lumps. You can also add chopped coriander leaves and green chilies for extra flavor. Let the batter rest for about 10-15 minutes. During this time, the semolina will absorb the liquid and thicken the batter.

Prepare the Steamer

Meanwhile, grease the idli plates or molds with a little oil to prevent sticking. Fill the bottom of the steamer with water and bring it to a boil

Steam the Idlis:

Once the batter has rested, pour it into the greased idli plates, filling each mold about 3/4th full. Place the idli plates in the steamer and cover it with a lid. Steam the idlis on medium heat for about 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. ingredients

1 cup semolina (rava)

1 cup yogurt (curd)

1/2 cup water

1/2 teaspoon mustard seed

1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds

1 tablespoon oil

1/2 teaspoon baking soda

Salt to taste

Finely chopped coriander leaves (optional)

Finely chopped green chilies (optional)

Grated carrot or coconut (optional, for garnish)

tools

Mixing bow

Steamer or idli maker

Idli plates or molds

Spatula or spoon

Small frying pan
